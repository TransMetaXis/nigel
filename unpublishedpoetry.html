What Is My Unpublished Poetry?

The following works are some of my creative expressions during the years of approximately 2020-2022. Many of them are inspirational and shows my intent for a higher more aligned level for our species and planet. Yet some of them have to do with the Covid-19 disease, the vaccination agenda, the lockdowns and our response to it both by the governments of the world and the response from the people as well. I voice my frustrations in great detail all in poetry form and near the tail end of that burst of creative inspiration I write about the trucker protest as well. These poems were my main method of getting my message across but it wasn't all doom and gloom. Through times of darkness, a light can always be found that one may focus on for hope, love, peace and joy to arrive.

Notable
When all seems lost in this day and age
When we don't like the looks of this freshly turned page
When the world feels as though we're sinking down
When we sense something really wrong in cities and towns

It lets me learn a quote by the late Bob Marley
One love, One heart, One destiny
I think about this quote like the three are connected
And when I look at anyone I see myself reflected

It gives me one from the late Mark Twain
Wrinkles should merely indicate where the smiles have been
That makes me think of time and how laugh lines are made
And memories of happiness that never have to fade

I discover a quote from the late Bertrand Russell
To be happy you first accept that the world is horrible
This one speaks to all of us right now it's very poignant
That doesn't mean our lives must go without enjoyment

This teaches me of one from the late Joseph Campbell
Following bliss, doors open where there were only walls
This is advice I'm sure a lot of us need
To see where the new worlds and vistas will lead

I try in my own way to honor these heroes
To share what their wisdom reveals and shows
Maybe to live it not write it is a better way to go
Would honor them more and the words they bestow

64
I hold my tongue
Until we've won
Til then I must
Hold fast and trust

In mankind's good
As well I should
With spirit strong
We reach beyond

Since in this tale
We shall not fail
We will not bail
We won't derail

I hear the sound
From out of bounds
It's getting near
Nothing to fear

Public Safety
With all of us social distancing and ordered to wear masks
Something's been on my mind so I really have to ask
If I got the common cold with fever, sniffles and the rest
Would I test positive for a covid-19 test?
You don't hear much about the common cold these days
Lately everyone who's sick has covid so they say
Does it not make you wonder where the cold and flu have gone?
And if you bring that up you're told obey because you're wrong
Whenever I acquiesce to rules of constriction
I feel like I dishonor all the truth of my convictions
Remember that I've touched only on one point of this scam
There's also death rates vaccines hospitals the list goes on god damn
Perhaps you don't appreciate this poem not one bit
Well I try to help the veil to lift but if not so be it

Scales to Tip
I got a funny feeling this will all work out
A feeling that the human race will find another route
Because if I continue feeling pangs of nagging doubt
I'll give in to ignoring what this life is all about

There's so much beauty I can find in this world of ours
Many people lifting consciousness in order to empower
Even though authority will loom above us like dark towers
They regardless have the time to be at peace or smell the flowers

I think of myself at times to walk the line of good and bad
Based on how I hope and dream or based on failures I have had
This type of balance that I give myself will tend to make me sad
Feeling I have so much work to do so much more good to add

But enough of that I think I'll think about more pleasant stuff
Cause I know that lately my mindset has been a little rough
Just don't test me on how good I feel you might just call my bluff
Okay I feel as though my mood has elevated just enough

If you made it this far thank you cause your eyes they mean a lot
Let us take a deep breath and let go of all these fights weve fought
Concentrate on all the love within your life and all it's brought
That could bring us closer to a world like that or so I've thought

Pro-Choice
I had mentioned a terminal case
In my previous vaccine poem
Of a mother who's life was erased
Leaving her kids to grow up alone

I mention it now first because
You might not roll back to the reference
Well she died from the vaccine which was
Pressured to her not her preference

The vaccine is safe for the public
Tell that to the lives that are lost
Vaccine deaths right now are the subject
Let's do our social duty but at what cost

You can tell me all you want
About there being a small chance
That one will die from the death shot
In spite of that I hold my stance

That fact that one needs to concede
That there's a chance that one could die
Makes any defense of the vaccine
Absolutely nullified

I said that I was gonna be mature
And not get emotionally charged
But my vaccine view is secure
Any science logic I will fully disregard

Because We All Know That It's True
That You Can Die From The Vaccines
Think I'm scared of covid I'm 42
And have no co-morbidities

And don't you tell me I endanger
When I know I'm in good health
Cause a fully vaxxed stranger
Wouldn't even know he's spreading it himself

And say I end up asymptomatic
well then I guess that's same as being vaxxed
Except I didn't need to risk traumatic
Death and that's a fact

What's with all the hyperbole
All it is is a pin prick
This is my right to choose personally
